Field Sales Managers
Regional and market-based sales representatives working externally to drive B2B and direct bookings
For Discussion
✅ What we know
✓A field sales function is required to grow the B2B agent network and corporate client base
✓Field Sales Managers would report into the CCO or BU Directors depending on territory
✓Key markets need localised field presence — India regions, international markets TBC
✓Field Sales typically operates on a base + commission structure with territory targets
❓ What is not yet defined
✗No Field Sales Manager roles currently defined in the structure
✗Territory split not yet agreed — geographic vs vertical (GIT/MICE/FIT) model
✗Relationship with B2B Account Managers inside BUs not yet defined
✗Headcount and cost model not yet approved
💬 Key discussion questions
Q1Should Field Sales be a central function under CCO, or embedded in each BU?
Q2What is the territory model — geographic (North/South/East/West India + International) or by BU vertical?
Q3How does a Field Sales Manager hand off to a B2B Account Manager once an agent is registered?
Q4What CRM and pipeline tools will Field Sales use?
Q5Commission structure — override on bookings sourced, or pure relationship/acquisition?
💡 Suggested approach (indicative only)
Suggested starting structure: National Field Sales Head → Regional Field Sales Managers ×4–6 (North India, South India, West India, East India, International, Tier 2 Cities). Each FSM owns agent acquisition and activation in their territory, handing registered agents to internal B2B Account Managers. Reporting line: CCO.

TripFactory Travel Agencies
Branded or franchise retail travel agency locations operating under or affiliated with TripFactory
Needs Definition
✅ What we know
✓TripFactory Travel Agencies concept exists — retail-facing booking points
✓Agencies would drive B2C bookings for GIT departures and FIT custom travel
✓Could operate as owned locations, franchise model, or preferred agent partnerships
✓Would need product access, booking system integration and brand guidelines
❓ What is not yet defined
✗Ownership model not defined — TripFactory-owned vs franchise vs white-label partner
✗Location strategy not confirmed — which cities/markets get physical agency presence
✗Revenue model not defined — does TripFactory take a margin, franchise fee, or both
✗How agency-sourced bookings flow to internal BU Booking Owners not defined
✗Technology and booking system access for agencies not scoped
💬 Key discussion questions
Q1Is this a company-owned retail model or a franchise/partner model?
Q2Which cities are prioritised for Phase 1 — Metro cities only, or Tier 2 as well?
Q3How does an agency-sourced booking flow through to an internal BU Booking Owner?
Q4What is the commercial model — agency earns commission on net rate, or sells at retail?
Q5How are agency staff trained, certified and quality-controlled against TripFactory policy?
💡 Suggested approach (indicative only)
Two models to evaluate: (1) Owned Retail — TripFactory opens branded offices in key cities, staffed with certified Experts as walk-in booking centres. (2) Franchise/Partner — TripFactory licenses the brand and booking system to independent travel agents under a signed franchise agreement with minimum sales commitments and full policy certification. Model 2 has lower capex but requires strong governance.

Global Offices
International office presence outside India — source markets, destination support, or regional operations
For Discussion
✅ What we know
✓TripFactory operates destinations globally — presence near key source markets may be needed
✓Marketing has confirmed localised offices per region — these may co-locate with Global Offices
✓Destination Management already has a global contracting model (360 contracts)
✓Some destinations (Europe, Australia, CIS) may benefit from local operational presence

✗Staffing model for Global Offices not defined — local hires vs India-based expats
✗Legal and entity structure for international offices not scoped
✗Budget for international office establishment not approved
💬 Key discussion questions
Q1Which markets justify physical Global Office presence vs virtual/remote coverage?
Q2What functions would a Global Office serve — sales, operations, contracting, or all three?
Q3Should Global Offices report to the Global CEO or to the relevant BU Director?
Q4What is the priority order — Singapore, Dubai, London, or Sydney first?
Q5What are the legal entity and employment law requirements for each priority market?

Phase 1 recommendation (indicative): Start with virtual/remote presence using a local representative model — 1 commercial representative per key source market (UK/Europe, UAE/Middle East, Australia/ANZ, Singapore/South East Asia). Each rep is a contractor or local hire, manages key travel agent and corporate relationships, and feeds into the India-based BU teams. Full office establishment in Phase 2 once volume justifies fixed costs.

Transport Operations
Coach fleet, private transfers, driver management and ground transport across all business units
Needs Definition
✅ What we know
✓Transport is confirmed as a Central Function with a Director role (TBC)
✓GIT requires group coaches for departures — potentially 30–200+ pax per departure
✓MICE requires event transfers, shuttle services and airport transfers
✓FIT requires private transfers, chauffeur services and small group vehicles
✓Driver float process exists — LERMS entries, float issue and 48hr expense settlement
❓ What is not yet defined
✗In-house fleet vs contracted fleet vs per-trip outsource model not decided
✗Driver employment model — employed, contracted, or third-party operators
✗Transport Director has not been identified or hired
✗Whether Transport is a profit centre or a cost centre not defined
✗Technology required — GPS tracking, driver app, LERMS integration
✗How transport pricing is handled — included in package vs charged to BUs
💬 Key discussion questions
Q1Is Transport in-house, outsourced, or a hybrid model by BU type?
Q2Who manages driver relationships day-to-day — Transport Director, BU Ops, or Central Ops?
Q3How does the Transport Director interact with GIT Ops TL, MICE Ops TL and FIT Ops TL?
Q4Is the coach contracting function part of Transport or Commercial Product?
Q5Does Transport own the driver float process end-to-end, or does Finance own it?
Q6How are international destination transfers managed — through DMC contracts or Transport?
Q7What technology is needed — fleet management, GPS tracking, driver mobile app?
💡 Suggested approach (indicative only)
Three-tier model to discuss: (1) Domestic long-haul and group coaches — contracted with 2–3 preferred coach operators under annual framework agreements managed by Transport Director. (2) City and airport transfers — mix of owned vehicles for high-frequency routes and contracted local operators. (3) International destination transfers — managed through DMC contracts in Commercial Product, coordinated by FIT/MICE Ops. Transport Director oversees all SLAs and driver compliance. Driver float owned by Transport, reconciled through Finance/LERMS.
